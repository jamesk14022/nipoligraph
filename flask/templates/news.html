What We Report

This page scans a range of online news outlets for articles mentioning any of the politicians that are tracked on this site. The insight that can be gained from this is currently limited, as a lot of news mentions involve only short quotes from party leaders or Executive Ministers, but we can at least see the visibility that each party and politician has in the media.

NB: NewsRiver is currently down, so news data have not updated since 25 September 2020.

News article volume

This tracks the number of online news articles each week (smoothed), as collected by NewsRiver, that include a mention of a member of one of the parties. See Technical Stuff for more explanation.
Party leaders and Assembly Ministers tend to dominate the mentions, as can be seen in the list of articles below.

News article sentiment

Each news article mentioning a politician is measured for its sentiment, or positivity; results shown are from May 2020 onwards. The aim here is to identify parties that are reported in a more positive or negative light, on average; but note that some of the articles are directly related to Ministries held by each party, which will affect the estimation of sentiment. See Technical stuff for more explanation.

Technical stuff

  • News data
  • Article sentiment
News article mentions are collected by searching for the politicians' names in the NewsRiver API. NewsRiver tracks a range of thousands of online news sources, including many focused on the UK and Ireland. Some additional filtering is done to remove false positives (articles mentioning someone with the same name as one of the politicians), but some incorrect detections may nevertheless exist in the article list.
In the volume plot, weekly volumes by party are smoothed with a 7-week running mean, after removing duplicates by party (so that a single article mentioning two members of the same party counts as only one mention).
NewsRiver returns a sentiment score for most articles, but I found this not to be very accurate, so instead use the excellent R package sentimentr. This takes a relatively sophisticated approach to determining text sentiment, but nevertheless relies on a vocabulary of words with pre-defined positive or negative sentiment associated. From this standard vocabulary I excluded some words that should be regarded as neutral in a political context, such as 'economic', 'justice', and 'conservative'. However, news article 'sentiment' remains hard to define: for example, an article describing a minister's response to an economic crisis will, almost unavoidably, be scored as 'negative', but the article may not comment on the efficacy or otherwise of the minister's actions. Therefore, this plot should be viewed with caution.
